Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Gyanloop

How is Gyanloop different from a traditional degree? +
We focus on job-ready skills, not just theory. Our curriculum is built with industry practitioners who bring real-world experience. You'll build a portfolio of 50+ hands-on projects, get 1 year of industry immersion, and graduate as a Senior Software Engineer or AI/ML Engineer — not a fresher.
What makes the CS & AI programme different from AI & Business? +
CS & AI is for builders — you'll dive deep into algorithms, system design, AI/ML, and algo trading. AI & Business is for product-minded technologists — you'll learn to build AI products, think like a founder, and lead cross-functional teams. Both include startup building and industry immersion.
Do I need prior coding experience to apply? +
Not at all. We start from the absolute basics and build up. What we care about is your curiosity, problem-solving ability, and drive to build things. Our curriculum is designed to take you from zero to industry-ready in 4 years.
What is the admission process like? +
Our process is designed to identify talent and potential. It includes a skills assessment to evaluate logical reasoning and analytical thinking, followed by a personal interview where we get to know your goals and passion for technology.
What kind of career support do you provide? +
We offer 1-on-1 mentorship, portfolio building, mock interviews, and career coaching. Our focus is on making you a standout candidate. You also get 6-12 months of paid industry immersion before graduation to gain real-world experience.
Is Gyanloop a college? Will I get a degree? +
We are not a college — we are a tech skills accelerator. You'll graduate with a portfolio of 50+ projects, industry certifications, and a network of mentors. Our focus is on employability and real-world impact, not degrees.
When will the next batch start? +
Our next cohort begins in August 2026. Applications are open now. Early applicants get access to pre-course material and mentorship sessions before the programme officially starts.
